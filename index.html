Tasty is a digital food and recipe brand known for its engaging cooking videos, recipes, and cooking tips. It offers a wide range of recipes, from quick and easy meals to more elaborate dishes, appealing to various tastes and dietary preferences. The brand also encompasses a website and mobile app where users can explore recipes, save their favorites, and even create personalized meal plans.

  • Over 3000 recipes
  • Shows ingredients and preparations
  • shows tricks and tips around the kitchen

SideChef is a cooking app designed to assist users at every stage of their cooking journey, from meal planning and grocery shopping to cooking and plating. The app offers a vast collection of recipes, ranging from simple home-cooked meals to gourmet dishes, catering to various dietary preferences and restrictions. It provides step-by-step instructions, photos, and videos to guide users through each recipe.

  • Over 16000 recipes
  • Shows how much time it would take to make a recipe

Excel is a powerful spreadsheet program developed by Microsoft, widely used for data analysis, organization, and calculation. Excel allows users to create complex calculations, charts, and graphs to visualize data trends and patterns. It also offers features such as pivot tables for summarizing large datasets, data validation for ensuring data accuracy, and conditional formatting for highlighting important information.

Why chefs use excel:

  • Recipe Development: Organize and manage recipes with ingredients, quantities, and instructions.
  • Cost Analysis: Calculate the cost of recipes to analyze profitability and adjust pricing.
  • Menu Planning: Plan menus for events or seasons, streamline ordering, and track ingredients.
  • Inventory Management: Track ingredient inventory levels, minimize waste, and avoid stockouts.
  • Scheduling and Staff Management: Create staff schedules, track working hours, and manage labor costs.
  • Menu Engineering: Analyze menu item performance based on sales data and profitability.
  • Communication and Collaboration: Facilitate communication and collaboration among kitchen staff and suppliers.

Sous vide is a cooking technique that involves cooking food in vacuum-sealed bags at precise and consistent temperatures in a water bath. By cooking food at a controlled temperature for an extended period, sous vide ensures even cooking and retention of moisture, resulting in tender and flavorful dishes. After cooking, the food can be finished by searing, grilling, or broiling to achieve a desired crust or texture on the exterior.

A wireless cooking thermometer is a kitchen tool designed to monitor the temperature of food while it's being cooked, providing convenience and precision

Benefits of Using a Wireless Thermometer for Cooking:

  • Remote Monitoring: Wireless thermometers allow you to monitor the temperature of your food remotely.
  • Precision: Many wireless thermometers provide accurate and precise temperature readings, ensuring that meats are cooked to the desired level of doneness.
  • Multi-Tasking: With a wireless thermometer, you can monitor the temperature of multiple dishes simultaneously.
  • Alerts and Notifications: Wireless thermometers often come with built-in alarms or notifications that alert you when your food has reached the desired temperature.
  • Convenience: Wireless thermometers eliminate the need to constantly check on your food, allowing you to focus on other tasks or enjoy time with your guests while cooking.

A smart oven is an advanced kitchen appliance equipped with intelligent features and connectivity capabilities that enhance cooking convenience, efficiency, and control.

Reasons to Use a Smart Oven:

  • Convenience: Smart ovens like June offer convenience with features such as remote control and monitoring via a smartphone app, allowing you to adjust settings and monitor cooking progress from anywhere.
  • Precision Cooking: June utilizes advanced cooking technology, such as built-in cameras and sensors, to automatically recognize food and suggest optimal cooking settings, resulting in precise and consistent cooking results.
  • Versatility: June ovens often come with a variety of cooking modes and presets for different types of food, from baking and roasting to air frying and dehydrating, making them versatile appliances for various cooking needs.
  • Time Savings: With features like fast preheating and accelerated cooking times, smart ovens like June can help you save time in the kitchen, allowing you to prepare meals more efficiently.
  • Energy Efficiency: Smart ovens are designed with energy-saving features, such as precise temperature control and efficient heating elements, to help reduce energy consumption during cooking.
  • Smart Integration: Many smart ovens, including June, integrate with other smart home devices and platforms, allowing you to automate cooking tasks, create custom recipes, and receive cooking recommendations based on your preferences.
