Part A

Tasty is a digital food and recipe brand known for its engaging cooking videos, recipes, and cooking tips. It offers a wide range of recipes, from quick and easy meals to more elaborate dishes, appealing to various tastes and dietary preferences. The brand also encompasses a website and mobile app where users can explore recipes, save their favorites, and even create personalized meal plans.

  • Over 3000 recipes
  • Shows ingredients and preparations
  • shows tricks and tips around the kitchen

SideChef is a cooking app designed to assist users at every stage of their cooking journey, from meal planning and grocery shopping to cooking and plating. The app offers a vast collection of recipes, ranging from simple home-cooked meals to gourmet dishes, catering to various dietary preferences and restrictions. It provides step-by-step instructions, photos, and videos to guide users through each recipe.

  • Over 16000 recipes
  • Shows how much time it would take to make a recipe
  • Shows how much cost per serving
  • Shows nutrition information for the recipe

Excel is a powerful spreadsheet program developed by Microsoft, widely used for data analysis, organization, and calculation. Excel allows users to create complex calculations, charts, and graphs to visualize data trends and patterns. It also offers features such as pivot tables for summarizing large datasets, data validation for ensuring data accuracy, and conditional formatting for highlighting important information. Many chefs around the world use excel to keep track of their recipes or to track of what they are buying or creating their prep list and much more.

Part B

For this part I thought that in the future there may be something like a holographic device that you could use to help you in the kitchen with stuff like recipes and how to prepare your ingredients before cooking.

Here's a list of the idea I have for this device:
  • Shows you recipes or a recipe video. (
  • Shows the nutrition facts, ingredients, and how long it would take in a preview before actually selecting the recipe to show.
  • Step you need to take to make it with images of how it should look like. (only for recipes without video)
  • Voice recognition
    • If the recipe has a video to it you can tap on the video and it will select the video and discard the recipe.
    • Saying stop will stop the video for you to catch up with what is going now.
    • Saying play will plays the video where it stopped.
    • Saying slow down will slow down the video.
